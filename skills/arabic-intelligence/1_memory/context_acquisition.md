# Thinking Model: Context Acquisition (فهم السياق وبناء النموذج الذهني)

## Concept
Before you generate a single word of Arabic UX copy, you MUST run this mental model. Do not write blindly. Do not assume. Build a cognitive profile of the user and the project first.

## The 4-Pillar Questionnaire

When a user asks you to write UX copy, silently answer these 4 questions in your `<thinking>` block:

### 1. The Project Context (What is this?)
*   Is it an E-commerce store? A FinTech app? A Government portal? A media blog?
*   *Action:* Based on this, route yourself to the correct file in `1_brand_engines/`.

### 2. The User Persona (Who is reading this?)
*   Are they a Gen-Z gamer? A CEO looking for B2B SaaS? A citizen checking their taxes?
*   *Action:* Adjust the tone. (Gen-Z = Conversational/Punchy. CEO = Logical/ROI-focused. Citizen = Respectful/Clear).

### 3. The Emotional State (How do they feel RIGHT NOW?)
*   **High Stress:** (Error screens, payment failures, complex forms). 
    *   *Rule:* Be extremely clear, calm, and provide an immediate solution. No jokes.
*   **Low Stress / Bored:** (Empty states, waiting screens). 
    *   *Rule:* Be engaging, motivate them, maybe add a touch of wit (if the brand allows).
*   **High Motivation:** (Checkout page, signing up). 
    *   *Rule:* Get out of their way. Remove friction. Use action-oriented CTAs.

### 4. The Goal of the Copy (What should they do next?)
*   UX copy is functional. It must drive action. What is the one thing the user needs to click or understand after reading this?
*   *Action:* Ensure the CTA or the guidance text directly supports this goal without ambiguity.

## Example of Context Acquisition in `<thinking>`
```xml
<thinking>
1. Project: B2B SaaS Platform for HR. (Route to Conversion/B2B Engine).
2. User: HR Managers and CEOs. They value time and compliance.
3. Emotional State: They are on a complex pricing page. They are probably calculating costs and feeling analytical/hesitant.
4. Goal: Convince them to book a demo.
5. Strategy: Use clear, ROI-focused language. Avoid slang. The CTA should reduce the perceived effort of booking.
</thinking>
```
